Sign Language

Sign Language is a visual language formed by organized hand gestures, body movements, and facial expressions. Its growing popularity highlights the demand for non-verbal communication that enriches confidence in communicating with people in everyday scenarios. The ASL alphabet (a.k.a fingerspelling) is one of the easier challenges when learning sign language and it’s a sign language basic necessary to know in order to succeed.

With this project we aim to reduce the knowledge gap between sign and non-sign user people. We want everyone to communicate freely without any boundaries.

Some facts about
Sign Language



  1. Facts


    1. It is not universal. It varies from country to country.
    2. It can use one or both hands. ASL uses 1 hand whereas, German or British Sign Language uses both hands.
    3. Sign languages have their own definite grammar. For instance, a well constructed question must be accompanied by the correct eyebrow position.
    4. Sign language doesn’t only use signs to communicate. It uses facial expression, hand movement and position,gestures and body language to communicate.
    5. Direction of the palm can change the entire meaning of the sign.
    6. In ASL, all the signs pertaining to women (such as wife, daughter etc.) are signed near to the jawline. On the other hand, men related signs (such as father, boy etc.) are signed by the forehead.
    7. Many deaf people have ‘name signs’. Instead of signing out the individual letters of their name, many people who communicate through sign language use a single sign to represent it. This ‘name sign’ is unique to the individual and can be compared to a nickname.
    8. Sign language isn’t as difficult to learn as it looks.
